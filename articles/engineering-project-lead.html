What to expect from an engineering project lead

By Winda Sekar Dewi

An Engineering Project Lead (EPL) is a crucial team member responsible for spearheading and managing engineering projects across teams and tribes. Their primary goal is to ensure timely completion of projects while meeting company objectives and maintaining high-quality deliverables. To excel in this role, an EPL should develop and refine the following key skills:

Strategic project planning and execution

In collaboration with Technical Program Managers, EPLs craft comprehensive project plans. These plans include detailed task breakdowns, clear project goals, deliverables, milestones, and realistic timelines based on thorough capacity estimations. EPLs ensure project completion within scheduled timeframes and work closely with cross-functional teams — including product management and design — to maintain overall project alignment.

Effective leadership and communication

EPLs must excel in communication to successfully lead projects. This involves articulating project plans, timelines, and progress updates to various stakeholders, including executive leadership and cross-functional teams. Skillful expectation management and regular, transparent communication about project progress and potential issues are essential.

In-depth technical expertise

A strong technical background is crucial for EPLs. They should be able to comprehend complex architectures and designs based on technical specifications. EPLs are also responsible for ensuring code quality adheres to best practice standards, resulting in reliable, scalable, and maintainable systems.

Proactive observability and risk management

Given the complexity of various projects, EPLs must be adept at identifying potential risks and bottlenecks early. This involves monitoring key metrics such as error rates, throughput, and latency. EPLs should develop robust contingency plans to mitigate risks effectively, ensuring successful project completion despite challenges.

Innovative research and development

To deliver high-quality projects, EPLs should stay current with emerging technologies and approaches. Regular evaluation of project performance through monitoring metrics and user feedback — using tools like Datadog and Mixpanel — is essential. Categorizing improvements into short-, mid-, and long-term solutions aids in prioritization and planning, and fosters a culture of continuous improvement.
