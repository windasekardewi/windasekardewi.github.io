Program Management

Cross-team project template

By Winda Sekar Dewi

Managing a project across multiple teams requires a structured approach that keeps everyone aligned without creating unnecessary overhead. This template covers the three phases of a cross-team project: planning, implementation, and go-to-market.

Phase 1: Planning

Action items

  • Dedicated sections for listing action items and assigning them to specific team members
  • Priority levels and due dates to ensure critical tasks are completed on time
  • Status updates and comments for ongoing communication and collaboration

Timeline management

  • Gantt charts or timelines for visual representation of the project schedule
  • Milestone tracking to highlight key dates and deadlines
  • Dependency management to map the relationship between tasks and teams

Capacity allocation

  • Monitor and allocate team capacity to ensure optimal resource utilization
  • Visual dashboard showing team workloads and availability
  • Balance workload distribution to prevent burnout and maintain project efficiency

Progress tracking

  • Track the status of each sub-project within the larger initiative
  • Visual indicators for completed, in-progress, and pending tasks

Kick-off meeting

After the planning phase, run a kick-off meeting with all project members to align stakeholders and set clear expectations. The agenda should cover:

  1. Introduction — welcome the team, walk through the agenda, introduce the project manager and key leads. Use a RACI matrix to clarify responsibilities.
  2. Background — explain why the project is being undertaken and its importance to the organization.
  3. Goals and scope — define what is in and out of scope. Align on success criteria.
  4. Collaboration setup — agree on communication channels, checkpoint cadence, shared tools, key documents, and the overall timeline including critical paths and dependencies.

Phase 2: Implementation

Tracking progress

  • Choose a tracking tool — Jira or a shared spreadsheet — and stick to it
  • Update task statuses regularly: to-do, in progress, completed
  • Monitor milestone completion against the project timeline

Reporting

  • Generate weekly reports covering progress, key achievements, and any issues encountered
  • Include highlights (what is going well) and lowlights (areas of concern or risk)
  • Keep stakeholders informed through consistent, structured updates

Dashboard visualization

  • Create a Jira dashboard with charts, graphs, and status indicators
  • Ensure the dashboard reflects real-time data so stakeholders can self-serve on project status

Phase 3: Go-to-market

Technical readiness

  • Confirm all technical components are tested and validated
  • Verify that dependencies are resolved and integrations are functioning as expected
  • Complete final quality assurance checks

Deployment strategy

  • Build a detailed deployment plan with clear steps for launch
  • Identify potential risks and prepare mitigation strategies
  • Plan rollback procedures in case of deployment issues

Stakeholder communication

  • Communicate the go-live schedule to all stakeholders in advance
  • Provide clear instructions and documentation to end-users and support teams
  • Set up support channels for immediate post-deployment assistance

Documentation

  • Ensure all project documentation is complete and accessible
  • Create user guides, technical manuals, and troubleshooting references
  • Archive project artifacts for future reference and compliance

Monitoring and support (optional)

  • Establish monitoring protocols to track system performance post-deployment
  • Allocate resources for immediate issue resolution
  • Schedule follow-up reviews to assess the deployment's success and address remaining issues
